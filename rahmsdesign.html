Dieter Rams
10 Principles of Design

1.
Good design is innovative


Whether a design embraces new technology, has an inventive form, or introduces a new function, design should always seek innovation.

2.
Good design makes a product useful


The function of an object is crucial to the usefulness for the user. If an object has dozens of uses but none are functional to the user, then the design is a failure.

3.
Good design is aesthetic


Appearence is relative to function. Appearence is also a first impression. If the user isn't drawn to the object, it is not useful.

4.
Good design makes a product understandable

Clear intent and honesty is important in product design, however I think that understandability is subjective especially with digital products. Depending on the service/use, I think that some objects should allow the user to explore the product to understand it.

5.
Good design is unobtrusive

In the digital realm, we can explore how "obtrusiveness" can be an element that adds complexity to a product.

6.
Good design is honest

Honesty is a quality for objects for daily use. However, I think there is room for hidden cleverness, especially in web/digital products.

7.
Good design is long-lasting

Sustainability is important to objects that are special to the user, however there are objects that should be disposable (I.E. packaging) and an ethical life cycle should be considered. Also, I believe that contemporary design should be catered to specific groups so those objects will remain relevant for a life-time.

8.
Good design is thorough down to the last detail

A fully realized design is one that considers the needed functions, relevant styles, cultural context, etc. Often times designs are created from moodboards and lack research/prototyping.

9.
Good design is environmentally friendly

10.
Good design is as little design as possible
